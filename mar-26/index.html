Humanitarian Mission by SPK Indonesia
*click the image for the details
We are pleased to share that CBCS has contributed Rp 2,000,000 to support the humanitarian mission organized by Perkumpulan Sekolah SPK Indonesia.
Through this collective mission of hope, assistance will be extended to our brothers and sisters in Cisarua, who have recently been affected by devastating natural disasters resulting in the loss of homes and stability. In addition, the program aims to support children in East Nusa Tenggara, where many still aspire to learn in classrooms that can adequately nurture their potential.
We believe that through collaboration and shared compassion, we can help restore hope, rebuild communities, and empower the next generation.

Tes Kemampuan Akademik (TKA) Tingkat SD (Grade 6 students)
The Academic Ability Test (TKA) is an assessment designed to measure students’ mastery of essential academic competencies as they prepare to move on to the next level of education. The test will cover core subjects such as Bahasa Indonesia, Mathematics, and other required subjects in accordance with current educational guidelines. The results will help the school map students’ academic readiness and determine any necessary follow-up support.
Reminder
Please remind your child of the following information:
  • Bringing money to school is not allowed
    For safety reasons, Grammar School students are not allowed to bring money and buy food from the canteen. If your child is often hungry after school, please provide him/her with sufficient, practical, healthy snacks, such as fruits, sandwiches, or crackers, to take to school.
  • Dismissal carpool
    CBCS has a “Quiet Policy” in the carpool area. This rule is for students and adults alike. This is not a time for conferences with the teacher. The teachers and students must be able to hear the carpool numbers when they are called. When a student hears/sees their family's number shown on the TV screen, they will go directly to the Front Office waiting for the car. Your child must be punctual to come to his/her shuttle car, school bus, or extracurricular class; therefore, we ask students not to buy food from the canteen.

Digital Citizenship Sessions
In today's connected world, our students need to cultivate responsible and ethical digital habits. The Upper School Counselor has led SOL Digital Citizenship Sessions to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and character needed to navigate the digital media responsibly.
Grounded in a biblical worldview, the sessions encourage students to honor God in their actions, uphold integrity, show kindness in online interactions, and exercise wisdom in their digital choices. As Scripture reminds us, “I have the right to do anything, you say, but not everything is beneficial. I have the right to do anything, but I will not be mastered by anything” (1 Corinthians 6:12, NIV).

Digital Citizenship Session
In today’s increasingly connected world, developing responsible and ethical digital habits is essential for our students. We are pleased to inform you that our school will be implementing a Digital Citizenship Session designed to equip students with the knowledge, skills, and discernment needed to navigate the online environment wisely and safely.
The program schedule is attached for your reference.
Below are the focus topics for each grade level:
  • Grade 10: Social Media & How You Feel
  • Grade 11: Misinformation, AI Deep Fake, and Media Literacy
  • Grade 12: Digital Footprint & Future Opportunities
We encourage parents to review the schedule and continue these important conversations at home. By working together, we can help our students become thoughtful, responsible, and discerning digital citizens. Thank you for your partnership in guiding our students toward wise and responsible technology use.
